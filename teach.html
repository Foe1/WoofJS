"I'm done with Scratch," our students tell us. "Now what?"

We've been trying to answer this question for the past two years: what's the programming language to learn after Scratch?

Scratch is amazing.

However, it has a ceiling that limits what types of games students can build. Multiplayer is impossible, and so are full screen and mobile games.

Because WoofJS commands directly correspond to Scratch blocks, they allow students to focus solely on syntax translation.


Going from move 10 steps in Scratch to car.move(10) in WoofJS is a small enough jump that students familiar with Scratch can pick it up in just a few hours.

When to switch your student to WoofJS?

Scratch feels too "childish"

Your student may be older or feel that they have outgrown Scratch. WoofJS may be particularly challenging for this type of student, but it can provide them with a more complex coding environment.

They want to create more complex projects

If your student is fighting against Scratch's limitations, it's time to switch to WoofJS. With WoofJS your student can build multi-player games, complex data structures, and mobile games.
